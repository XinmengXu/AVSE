Monaural speech enhancement with multi-head self and cross attention in the time domain

Accurate modeling of the long-term correlation of noisy speech is crucial to the performance of a speech enhancement system. This paper proposed a novel speech enhancement network, which combines a U-Net architecture with multi-head self- and cross-attention, for end-to-end speech enhancement in time domain. The proposed method overcomes the inability of U-Net to model long-term correlations and dependencies, which are crucial for accurate noisy speech modeling. For this purpose, multi-head attention mechanisms are incorporated at two levels: 1) a multi -head self-attention module leverages global interactions between encoder features, while 2) multi-head cross-attention in the skip connections allows a fines recovery in the decoder by filtering out uncorrelated feature. Experimental results illustrate that the proposed architecture obtains consistently better performance than recent models of both PESQ and STOI scores.

Dataset

  • We used the LibriSpeech dataset and DEMAND dataset.
